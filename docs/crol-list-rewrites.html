crol-list, before & after

readable-or-else is a readability CI gate — a Flesch-Kincaid grade-level check in CI that fails a pull request when a page misses its target reading level — plus an LLM-backed mode that rewrites failing text to a target grade, re-measures the rewrite with the same formula used for gating, and only surfaces it if it actually hits the target and preserves the original's numbers, links, and named entities. Below are the ten genuinely-hardest passages from crol-list’s about.html and data.html — crol-list's own reference pages, all measuring Flesch-Kincaid grade 9 or higher — run through a real LLM rewrite call each, not mocked or hand-edited.

“Always make your content as simple as you can without losing meaning. Try to simplify your content to a seventh grade reading level or lower. We use the Flesch–Kincaid grade level formula.” NYC Web Content Style Guide, “Reading level” — designsystem.nyc.gov/standards/nyc-web-content-style-guide.html — the nycsg7 preset's source, target grade 7.

Result: 4 of 10 rewrite calls landed at or under grade 7 on the first try and passed the entity-preservation check. The rest are shown too — a rewrite that stays over target is a real, honestly-reported outcome, not a failure to hide. See readable-or-else on GitHub.
